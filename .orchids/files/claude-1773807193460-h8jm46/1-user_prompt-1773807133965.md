1. Stamina (Time × Distance)
What it measures: Sustained cardiovascular effort
Scale
1 — Minimal Effort
• <2 hrs/day
• <5 km
2 — Light Effort
• 3–4 hrs/day
• 5–8 km
3 — Moderate Endurance
• 5–6 hrs/day
• 8–12 km
4 — High Endurance
• 7–9 hrs/day
• 12–18 km
5 — Extreme Endurance
• 10–14+ hrs/day
• 18 km+
2. Strength (Elevation Load + Joint Impact)
What it measures: Vertical effort + muscular/joint stress
Scale (Daily Elevation Change)
1 — Negligible Load
• <200m
2 — Light Load
• 200–500m
3 — Moderate Load
• 500–900m
4 — High Load
• 900–1400m
5 — Extreme Load
• 1400m+
3. Power (Anaerobic Burst)
What it measures: Explosive effort requirement
Scale
1 — None
• Continuous walking
2 — Occasional
• Stepping over obstacles
3 — Intermittent
• Short scrambling / boulder hopping
4 — Sustained
• Continuous scrambling / deep snow effort
5 — Technical
• Roped climbing / jumaring / ice work
4. Agility (Balance / Terrain Stability)
What it measures: Footing complexity
Scale
1 — Stable
• Flat, wide trails
2 — Uneven
• Rocky / root-filled trails
3 — Unstable
• Scree, moraine, patchy snow
4 — Highly Unstable
• Glacier travel, narrow ledges
5 — Extreme Precision
• Ice slopes, razor ridges

Rule:
• Default = 0 for all hikes/treks
Only rate if:
• Swimming required
• Significant river crossings with risk
PSYCHOLOGICAL DIMENSIONS
7. Nerve (Exposure / Fear Factor)
Scale
1 — None
• No drop-offs
2 — Mild
• Occasional exposure
3 — Moderate
• Noticeable drop-offs / bridges
4 — High
• Cliffs / glacier cracks
5 — Extreme
• Knife-edge ridges / crevasse crossings
8. Focus (Attention / Consequence of Error)
Scale
1 — Passive
• No need to watch footing
2 — Low Attention
• Occasional awareness
3 — Active Attention
• Must watch steps
4 — Continuous Vigilance
• Frequent slip risk
5 — Zero-Error Zone
• One misstep = fatal
